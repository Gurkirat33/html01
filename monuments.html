Qutub Minar Complex

The Majestic Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and has survived the ravages of time remarkably well. The complex includes:

  • The Minar (Tower): 73-meter high tower of victory
  • Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque: First mosque built in Delhi
  • Iron Pillar: 4th-century pillar that hasn't rusted
  • Alai Darwaza: Beautiful domed gateway

Humayun's Tomb

The Magnificent Humayun's Tomb

Often called the precursor to the Taj Mahal, Humayun's Tomb features:

  • Persian Architecture: Perfect blend of Persian and Indian styles
  • Char Bagh: Four-quartered garden layout
  • Multiple Tombs: Houses several Mughal family members
  • Water Channels: Intricate irrigation system

Other Notable Monuments

  • Jama Masjid: India's largest mosque
  • Safdarjung Tomb: Last enclosed garden tomb
  • Purana Qila: Oldest fort in Delhi
  • Jantar Mantar: 18th-century observatory
  • Agrasen ki Baoli: Ancient stepwell
